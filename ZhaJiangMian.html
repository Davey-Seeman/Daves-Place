Zha Jiang Mian

By Ellen - China

Origins: This cheap and casual dish originated in Beijing, China, but was quickly enbraced by Koreans as well when Shandong immigrants introduced it to them.
Source: https://www.tasteatlas.com/

  • Recipe:
    • Season minced meat with salt and pepper. Heat up a pan with oil and once hot, brown the meat in the pan with garlic.
    • After meat is brown, add soy sauce and hoisin sauce. Bring to a boil. When finished put it in a bowl.
    • Boil bokchoi in water. When finished, drain it in a stainer and put it in a bowl.
    • Boil noodles in a pot for 7-10 minutes. While they are boiling, whisk the eggs with salt, pepper, and milk in a bowl.
    • Fry the eggs in an oiled pan. When finished put in a bowl.
    • When noodles are finished, drain them and put them in a bowl.
    • Serve by combining the noodles with the meat topped by the scrambled eggs and bokchoi.

    Time to Cook:

    30 Mins

  • Ingredients:
    • 500g chicken or pork
    • Baby bokchoi
    • Thick noodles
    • 4 eggs
    • 2 cloves of garlic
    • 1 tsp soy sauce
    • 100ml hoisin sauce
    • 1 tsp vinegar
    • 2 tsp oil
    • A pinch of salt
    • A pinch of pepper
    • 1 tsp milk
