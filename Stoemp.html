Stoemp

By Alex - Belgium

Origins: Stoemp comes from Brussels, Belgium, and is one of Belgium's most popular meals. It was created in the 19th century when meat was scarce in Belgium.
Source: https://www.tasteatlas.com/

  • Recipe:
    • Cut celery root and cauliflower. Discard outer green leaves and rough stems. Soak florets in a bath of salty water for a few minutes.
    • Cut potatoes into chunks, roughly about the same size as the cauliflower florets and celery root so they cook evenly. Put potatoes, celery root and cauliflower into a large pot, and bring to a boil. Simmer until done and easily mashed.
    • In the meantime, cut the dark green tops off of the leeks and slice off the bottom root. Cut each stalk in half lengthwise and rinse under cold running water to remove any dirt. After, slice stalks into thin rings or strips.
    • Cut carrot and slice it into thick slices. Then, finely dice each slice into small pieces.
    • Melt 1 tbsp of butter in a pan and sauté carrots over medium-low heat. When carrot is starting to soften after 4-5 min, add leeks and continue to sauté until vegetables are soft, slightly browned and caramelized.
    • Sprinkle cumin over vegetables, and sauté an additional 2 minutes to release the flavor of the cumin.
    • Combine the cauliflower, celery root and potato mixture with the leek & carrot mixture. Add 2-3 tbsp of butter, 1 clove of minced garlic and 1 tbsp of salt & pepper.
    • Mash these ingredients together.
    • Sprinkle some grated cheese over the top and serve hot.

    Time to Cook:

    30 mins

  • Ingredients:
    • 1.5 lbs of yellow potatoes
    • 1/2 medium sized cauliflower
    • 3 leeks
    • 1 medium carrot
    • 3-4 tbsp of butter
    • 1 tsp of cumin
    • 1 tbsp of salt & pepper
    • Pecorino-Romano cheese, grated (optional)
