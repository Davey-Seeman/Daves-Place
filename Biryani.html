Biryani

By Josh - Sri Lanka

Origins: Supposedly inspired by Emperor Mumtaz Mahal, Biryani dates back to the Mughal Empire in Northern India in the 1600s.
Source: https://www.tasteatlas.com/

  • Recipe:
    • Marinate the chicken in yogurt, turmeric powder, salt, 2 chopped green chiles. Then set aside.
    • In a large pan fry 2 sliced onions, bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon, coriander powder, and cumin powder until onions are translucent.
    • In a separate bowl combine 1/4 bunch of chopped coriander, 2 chopped green chiles, chopped mint, 1 tsp of garlic paste, and 1 tsp of ginger paste.
    • Add these ingredients into the frying pan with the onions. Let it try for 10 mins on medium heat.
    • Add two chopped tomatoes, and cook for another 10-15 mins. Then add marinated meat, cover, and cook until the meat is tender (approx 30 mins)
    • In a large baking dish, add just the meat and 3 cups of washed basmati rice.
    • Then add 4.5 litres of the liquid that the meat was cooked in. If there is not enough liquid, add some water.
    • Cover baking dish with foil and bake for an hour at 350 degrees fahrenheit.

    Time to Cook:

    90 Mins

  • Ingredients:
    • 3 cups of rice
    • 3 pounds of chicken
    • 1/2 cup yogurt
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder
    • 4 green chiles (diced)
    • 2 sliced onions
    • 2 diced tomatoes
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 4 cloves
    • 1/4 bunch fresh coriander
    • fresh mint
    • cinnamon bark
    • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
    • 1/2 tsp cumin
    • 1 tsp ginger paste
    • 1 tsp garlic paste
    • 1/2 tsp salt
